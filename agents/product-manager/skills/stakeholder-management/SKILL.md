# Stakeholder Management Framework

A structured framework for identifying, analyzing, and managing stakeholders throughout the product lifecycle.

---

## When to Use This Skill

Use this skill when:
- Starting a new product/initiative
- Need to align multiple teams
- Managing conflicting priorities
- Preparing for major decisions
- Reporting progress to leadership

---

## Stakeholder Identification

### Internal Stakeholders

| Role | Interest | Influence | Engagement |
|------|----------|-----------|------------|
| **Executives** | Business outcomes | High | Monthly updates |
| **Product** | Product success | High | Weekly sync |
| **Engineering** | Technical feasibility | High | Daily collaboration |
| **Design** | User experience | Medium | Weekly sync |
| **Marketing** | Go-to-market | Medium | Bi-weekly sync |
| **Sales** | Revenue, customers | Medium | Monthly updates |
| **Support** | Customer issues | Low-Medium | Bi-weekly sync |
| **Legal/Compliance** | Risk management | Medium (for regulated) | As needed |

### External Stakeholders

| Role | Interest | Influence | Engagement |
|------|----------|-----------|------------|
| **Customers** | Value delivery | High (collective) | Continuous feedback |
| **Partners** | Integration, revenue | Medium | Monthly/Quarterly |
| **Investors** | Growth, returns | High (for startups) | Quarterly |
| **Analysts** | Market positioning | Medium | As needed |

## Stakeholder Analysis Matrix

### Power-Interest Grid

```
        High Power
            │
  ┌─────────┴─────────┐
  │  MANAGE CLOSELY   │  KEEP SATISFIED
  │  (Key Players)    │  (Keep informed,
  │                   │   meet needs)
────┼─────────┼─────────┼──── Interest
  │  KEEP INFORMED    │  MONITOR
  │  (Minimal effort) │  (No action needed)
  └─────────┴─────────┘
            │
        Low Power
```

### Categorization

| Quadrant | Stakeholders | Strategy |
|----------|--------------|----------|
| **Manage Closely** | Executives, Key customers | Engage frequently, involve in decisions |
| **Keep Satisfied** | Investors, Legal | Keep informed, meet their needs |
| **Keep Informed** | Marketing, Support | Regular updates, gather feedback |
| **Monitor** | General users, Partners | Minimal effort, watch for changes |

## Managing Difficult Stakeholders

### The Blocker

**Signs:**
- Constantly says "no"
- Raises objections late
- Withholds support

**Strategy:**
1. Understand their concerns (listen first)
2. Involve them early in decisions
3. Find common ground
4. Escalate only if necessary

---

### The Micromanager

**Signs:**
- Wants detailed updates
- Questions small decisions
- Hard to delegate to

**Strategy:**
1. Over-communicate proactively
2. Set clear boundaries
3. Show competence consistently
4. Give them ownership of something

---

### The Absent Stakeholder

**Signs:**
- Never responds to emails
- Skips meetings
- Disengaged

**Strategy:**
1. Find what matters to them
2. Make engagement easy (short, focused)
3. Escalate to their manager if critical
4. Document decisions made without them

---

### The Scope Creeper

**Signs:**
- Always adding "just one more thing"
- Changes priorities frequently
- Doesn't respect constraints

**Strategy:**
1. Show trade-offs explicitly
2. Use prioritization framework
3. Get commitments in writing
4. Involve their manager in prioritization

## Stakeholder Alignment Meeting Template

```markdown

## Purpose
{Why this meeting is happening}

## Attendees
{List of stakeholders}

## Agenda (60 min)
1. Context & Problem (10 min)
2. Proposed Solution (15 min)
3. Discussion (20 min)
4. Decision & Next Steps (15 min)

## Pre-Read
{Links to documents to review beforehand}

## Desired Outcomes
- [ ] Alignment on problem
- [ ] Alignment on solution approach
- [ ] Decision on {specific decision}
- [ ] Clear next steps

## Follow-Up
- Meeting notes sent within 24 hours
- Action items tracked
- Next check-in scheduled
```
