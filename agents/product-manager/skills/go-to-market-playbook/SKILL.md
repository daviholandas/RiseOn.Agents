# Go-to-Market Playbook

A comprehensive playbook for planning and executing go-to-market (GTM) strategies. Use this when launching products or entering new markets.

---

## When to Use This Skill

Use this skill when:
- Launching a new product
- Entering a new market
- Launching major features
- Expanding to new customer segments
- Planning product launches

---

## GTM Strategy Components

### 1. Target Market Definition

**Ideal Customer Profile (ICP):**
```
Company Characteristics:
- Industry: {industries}
- Size: {employee count/revenue}
- Location: {geography}
- Technology stack: {technologies used}

Buyer Personas:
- {Persona 1}: {role, pain points, buying criteria}
- {Persona 2}: {role, pain points, buying criteria}
```

**Market Sizing:**
```
TAM (Total Addressable Market): ${X}B
SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market): ${X}M
SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market): ${X}M (Year 1)
```

---

### 2. Value Proposition & Positioning

**Value Proposition:**
```
For [target customer]
Who [has this need/problem]
Our [product/service]
Is a [category]
That [key benefit]
Unlike [alternative]
We [unique differentiator]
```

**Positioning Statement:**
```
[Product] is the [category] that helps [target customer]
[achieve benefit] by [how it works].

Unlike [alternative], we [unique advantage].
```

**Messaging Pillars:**
| Pillar | Message | Proof Point |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| {Pillar 1} | {message} | {proof} |
| {Pillar 2} | {message} | {proof} |
| {Pillar 3} | {message} | {proof} |

---

### 3. Pricing Strategy

**Pricing Model:**
| Model | Description | Best For |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| **Subscription** | Recurring revenue | SaaS, services |
| **Usage-based** | Pay per use | APIs, infrastructure |
| **Freemium** | Free tier + paid | Consumer, PLG |
| **Per-seat** | Per user pricing | B2B tools |
| **Tiered** | Feature tiers | Most B2B SaaS |

**Price Points:**
```
Tier 1: ${X}/month - {features included}
Tier 2: ${X}/month - {features included}
Tier 3: Enterprise - Custom pricing
```

**Pricing Rationale:**
- Based on value metric: {what customer pays for}
- Competitive analysis: {positioning vs competitors}
- Willingness to pay: {from customer interviews}

---

### 4. Distribution Strategy

**Channels:**

| Channel | Type | Investment | Expected ROI |
|---------|------|------------|--------------|
| **Direct Sales** | Sales team | High | High (enterprise) |
| **Self-Service** | Website/app | Medium | Medium (SMB) |
| **Partner** | Resellers | Low | Medium |
| **Marketplace** | App stores | Low | Low-Medium |
| **Content/SEO** | Organic | Medium | High (long-term) |
| **Paid Ads** | Google/FB | High | Medium |

**Channel Prioritization:**
```
Primary: {channel 1} - {rationale}
Secondary: {channel 2} - {rationale}
Experimental: {channel 3} - {rationale}
```

### 5. Launch Plan

**Pre-Launch (4-6 weeks):**
| Week | Activity | Owner | Status |
|------|----------|-------|--------|
| -6 | Finalize messaging | Product Marketing | □ |
| -5 | Create sales enablement | Sales Enablement | □ |
| -4 | Beta launch | Product | □ |
| -3 | PR outreach | Marketing | □ |
| -2 | Training complete | All teams | □ |
| -1 | Final QA | Product | □ |

**Launch Week:**
| Day | Activity | Owner |
|-----|----------|-------|
| Monday | Internal announcement | CEO |
| Tuesday | Press release | Marketing |
| Wednesday | Customer emails | Marketing |
| Thursday | Social media push | Marketing |
| Friday | Launch retrospective | All |

**Post-Launch (4 weeks):**
| Week | Focus | Metrics |
|------|-------|---------|
| 1 | Monitor & fix issues | Bug count, support tickets |
| 2 | Gather feedback | NPS, user interviews |
| 3 | Optimize conversion | Sign-up rate, activation |
| 4 | Plan iteration | Feature requests, roadmap |
